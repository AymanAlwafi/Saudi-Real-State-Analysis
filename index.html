Classifying Saudi Real Estate with Machine Learning

A comparative analysis of Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, and Random Forest to predict property value categories ("Affordable" vs. "High-Value") aligned with Vision 2030 housing trends.

Data Methodology

1. Cleaning & Encoding

Handled Arabic text (UTF-8-SIG), encoded City names to integers, and managed missing values in critical columns.

2. Outlier Removal

Applied Interquartile Range (IQR) method to remove extreme property sizes (commercial/palaces) that skewed results.

3. Target Engineering

Converted continuous Price into a binary classification target: 0 (Affordable) vs. 1 (High-Value).

Decision Tree Results

A tree-based model tuned with Entropy criterion and Max Depth of 5.

83.99% Accuracy
Medium Generalization
Analysis: Better than baseline, but prone to overfitting without the ensemble averaging provided by Random Forest.

Random Forest Results (Winner)

An ensemble of 50 decision trees (Max Depth=10) voting on the outcome.

87.61% Accuracy
0.95 AUC Score
Analysis: The ensemble approach effectively handled the "Majlis" (Livings) feature and Location encoding, reducing variance and improving prediction stability.
